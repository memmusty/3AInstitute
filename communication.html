Activities

Study and Define Tic Tac Toe strategy

For this activity, I was paired with Ned. This activity involved playing tic tac toe repeatedly with Ned, consciously noting his and my moves and decipher our strategies for winning the game. We took turns in playing as the first player and paid attention to the move we make as the first player, the move we makes as the second player, how we try to win the games and block the opponent from winning. Tic Tac Toe is a game I have been playing since I was a child, and this activity was rather an interesting one. Paying attention how I reason, strategize and restrategize based on my opponent's move, and also how I study his moves, try to figure out his strategies and predict his next move to determine the best winning strategy was an eye-opener on what we might want to think about when we document or communicate strategies.

Interestingly, when we tried to redefining the strategy for audiences with no experience playing tic tac toe. It became obvious that there are some level of assumptions we have about our audiences prior knowledge and our audiences shape the language we use in communicating abstract ideas.

Takeaway Lessons

  • AI and Machine learning: Not only machine learning systems are AI systems. AI agents are not limited to learning and taking deicions using machine learning algorithms
  • Team work is important - especially with diverse groups, they bring in multiple perspective.
  • Detailed and proper documentation is important. When doing this, it is most important to put my audiences first, understand their language and have no assumptions about their prior knowledge or language.

Achievement

At the end of the activities, I:
  • Created an intelligent agents from ideating to programming
  • Collaboratively contributed to a project of diverse team
  • Understand what goes into creating a simple agent from idea to executable code - understand how human assumptions, intuition, and strategic thinking get translated into computational agents
  • Learned the act of active and detailed documentation of my journey, experience, decision, assumptions when developing algorithms and systems whether personal ot shared.
These exercises equipped me with communication skills which I think are essential for every individual, especially those that will be contributing technically to social problems. Detailed and proper documentation makes it easier for me or any other person to be able to pick up my project, understand my thoughts and design process, and modify it accordingly - it is like a letter to someone in the future (it can be oneself). As I go on to tackling social problems, I see myself applying this in my algorithm design process, when coding, and communicating or sharing my work with others.
