Build Portfolio

As I progress in my journey to become a professional cyberneticist at the 3AInstitute, I have learned new skills relevant to practitioners of applied cybernetics and improved on my skillset. This portfolio provides a summary of the top 3 key skills I have developed during the duration of the second semester of CECS 8001, a course that is designed to enable us understand - by building or tearing apart cyber-physical systems and question them through hands-on exercises. It provides brief reflections on my journey so far, lessons learned, challenges faced, and the exciting achievements I accomplished through participating in the course activities. It starts with a reflection on my experience conducting evidence-based system analysis, system risk assessment and management with STPA (Systems Theoretic Process and Analysis), and machine learning model development.



Key Skills Gained

  • Evidence-Based System Analysis

    This is a detailed documentation of my journey and reflection on conducting evidence-based system analysis. This involved framing research questions, modeling the system's (dynamic) behavior, conducting experiments, analyzing and reporting the exeriment result.

  • Risk Assessment and Management with STPA

    This provide an overview of my experience learning and conducting hazard analysis with STAMP STPA, understanding how autonomous vehicles function, coupling it with my experience as Lagos resident. My reflection on the STPA method, its percieved applicability and limitations are documented here.

  • Machine Learning from Scratch

    This page provides a documentation of my attempt to understand ML models by "tearing ML models apart", and to build a decision tree model from scratch. I will be discussing my progress, challenges, decision process and my thoughts on my approach to understanding ML models.
